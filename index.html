About

Currently, I am looking for jobs in the GIS/Geography field. I graduated from Binghamton University with a Masters Degree in Geography, focusing on GIS (Geographic Information Systems). I am interested in many aspects of geography such as urban planning, historical geography, cultural geography, and interactive web mapping. I received my Bachelors degree at SUNY Cortland where I studied GIS focusing on Advanced Geospatial Applications. Additionally I minored in Computer Applications, and Anthropology.

Growing up, I never quite knew what I wanted to do. I knew I loved art but that I wasn't greatest at it. At SUNY Cortland somehow geography just fell into my lap and I had finally realized what was missing. I see GIS not only as an information platform but as "art" as well. The map needs to be clear, concise, and aesthetically pleasing. I enjoy what I do and work very hard to put out "pretty" maps.

When I'm not making maps I enjoy traveling, and improving my photography skills. During my junior year of undergrad, I studied abroad for one semester in Australia and I'd like to think that it changed my life for the better. I was pushed into the unknown, was by myself and it really challenged me to grow as a person. I explored, made new friends, and learned more about the world around me. Upon my return I realized that I caught the travel bug. Every year I am determined to travel to either a new state or country. I figure there's a whole world out there so why not explore it.

GIS Technician/Analyst

January 2015 - August 2016

My first real paid job as a GIS Technician/Analyst. Suez partnered up with Nassau County for a 20 year contract (public/private partnership). I started in core team of 3, which then went on to be a core team of 5. I was in charge of improving and updating the geodatabase that was used for in-house and field crew use. I made hard copy maps and worked on the website that the company could access. I worked with previous vector data, and created new ones as projects came up. For privacy, the maps shown are base maps that divided the towns up by districts for office and field crew planning. Other projects that I worked on here include but are not limited to Sonar Inspection Planning, SSO History, Sewer Stoppage reportings, and more. I ended up leaving my position in August 2016 as I was given the opportunity to go to Graduate school with a scholarship and stipend offer. It was not an easy decision to make but I knew that I needed and wanted to learn more about GIS.
